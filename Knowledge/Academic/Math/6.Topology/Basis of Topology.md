Let $X$ be a non set empty set, and $\mathcal{B}$ is a collection of subsets of $X$. $\mathcal{B}$ is called a **basis** of $X$ if and only if the following conditions are satisfied:

$1.$ for all $x\in X$, there exist at least one $B\in \mathcal{B}$ such that $x\in B$

$2.$ For any $B_1,B_2\in \mathcal{B}$ and any $x\in X$.
if $x\in (B_1\cap B_2)$ then there exist $B_3\in \mathcal{B}$ such that $x\in B_3\subset (B_1\cap B_2)$.
