>[!def|*]
Let $\displaystyle X$ be a set, a sequence in $X$ is simply a function from $\displaystyle\mathbb{N}$ to $X$. If  $\displaystyle\varphi:\mathbb{N}\to X$ is a sequence, we write$$(x_n), \ (x_n)_{n\in\mathbb{N}},\ \\\\ \\ \\ \text{or   }\ \ (x_0,x_1,x_2,x_3\dots)$$

>[!example]
>$1.\left(1,\dfrac12,\dfrac13,\dfrac14,\dots\right)$.
>
>$2.\left(\dfrac{1+n}{n}\right)_{n=1}^{\infty}=\left(\dfrac21,\dfrac32,\dfrac43,\dots\right)$.
>
>$3.(a_n)$, where $(a_n)=2^n$ for each $n\in\mathbb{N}$.
