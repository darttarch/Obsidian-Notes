# Upper and Lower Bounds

> [!def]
> Let $S$ be a nonempty subset of $\mathbb{R}$.  
>
> **1.** If there exists a number $b \in \mathbb{R}$ such that $\forall s \in S,\ s \le b$,  
> then $b$ is called an **upper bound** of $S$.  
>
> **2.** If there exists a number $b \in \mathbb{R}$ such that $\forall s \in S,\ b \le s$,  
> then $b$ is called a **lower bound** of $S$.

---

# Least Upper Bound and Greatest Lower Bound

> [!def]
> Let $S \subset \mathbb{R}$ with $S \ne \varnothing$.  
>
> **1.** The **least upper bound** (or **supremum**) of $S$, denoted $\sup S$, is the smallest element among all upper bounds of $S$.  
>
> **2.** The **greatest lower bound** (or **infimum**) of $S$, denoted $\inf S$, is the largest element among all lower bounds of $S$.

# Maximum and Minimum:

>[!def]
>Let $S$ be a bounded subset of $\mathbb{R}$.
>$$
\begin{align}
\sup{S} \in S \implies \sup{S}=\max{S} \\
\inf{S}\in S\implies \inf{S}=\min{S}
\end{align}$$
